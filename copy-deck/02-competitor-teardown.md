## 01 — REVELO
**URL:** revelo.com
**Model:** Volume marketplace

### Hero headline
> "NEARSHORE TALENT PLATFORM FOR THE AI ERA"
> "FIND, HIRE & PAY ELITE ENGINEERS BASED IN LATAM"

### Subheadline
"LatAm's largest tech talent network of 400K+ pre-vetted engineers. Find the exact skills and experience you need, in your timezone. We handle payroll, benefits, taxes, and compliance."

### Primary CTA
"Hire Developers" / "Schedule a Call"
**Value prop under CTA:** "Get a curated shortlist in 72 hours. No upfront fees, no lock-ins."

### Color palette
| Hex | Role |
|---|---|
| `#18006A` | Primary brand / nav background |
| `#7455FF` | Accent / CTA / interactive elements |

### What to steal
**The 72-hour commitment.** They pin a specific, concrete delivery promise directly under the CTA. It's the thing that makes the buyer feel safe clicking. TMT SA LLC needs an equivalent concrete anchor — not "fast delivery" but a specific number tied to a specific outcome. Ours is quality, not speed, so the equivalent might be: "Top 3% pass rate. Zero attrition mandate." — specific, verifiable, attached to the CTA.

### What to exploit
**"400K+ pre-vetted" is the vulnerability.** That number is their proof of scale and simultaneously their credibility problem. A U.S. CTO who has been burned by a fast hire reads "400,000 pre-vetted engineers" and thinks "automated filter." Nobody reviews 400,000 humans. The "AI Era" framing compounds this — they're leaning into AI at exactly the moment CTOs are becoming skeptical of AI-filtered resumes. Their positioning says speed; ours says signal. Never mention their name — just build the contrast into the Paradigm Shift section.

**"No lock-ins" signals high churn.** You only advertise "no lock-ins" if your clients need an exit. It's an admission, not a feature.

**URL:** howdy.com
**Model:** Culture infrastructure / EOR hybrid

### Subheadline
"We help staff the top 1% LatAm engineers, train teams on AI, and make sure your company shows up where people are searching."

### Primary CTA
"Talk to Us"

### Color palette

### Font stack

### What to steal
**"Unfair Advantage" framing.** It's the best headline positioning in the competitive set. It flips the narrative — instead of selling a service, it promises an asymmetric outcome. The buyer isn't buying engineers; they're buying leverage their competitors don't have. TMT SA LLC should borrow this psychological frame without copying the words. Our version: the boutique access that volume factories can't offer IS the unfair advantage.

**"Top 1%" claim.** Specific percentage. Even if unverified, it anchors quality. We need our own percentage — and ours ("top 3% pass our architectural crucible") is actually more credible because it explains the mechanism.

### What to exploit
**The headline is vague and the subheadline is confused.** "Staff engineers + train teams on AI + make sure your company shows up where people are searching" — that's three different businesses in one sentence. SEO, staffing, and AI training have nothing to do with each other. The positioning is scattered. TMT SA LLC does one thing and does it with extreme precision. That focus IS the differentiator.

**"Talk to Us" is the weakest CTA in the set.** It signals nothing. No commitment, no outcome, no urgency. A U.S. CTO with 40 minutes of calendar space doesn't want to "talk." They want a specific outcome from a specific conversation. "Book a Discovery Call" with a Calendly link is 10x stronger.

**"Western Hemisphere" is oddly geographic.** Canada? Mexico? Brazil? It dilutes the LatAm precision positioning. TMT SA LLC's Ecuador-first specificity is actually sharper.

**URL:** icebergsolutions.com
**Model:** Legacy integrator / boutique

### Hero headline
> "Quality, security, and long-term reliability."
> "Making a positive impact through technology."

### Subheadline
"Boutique, owner-operated software engineering firm, with 30 years of experience delivering mission-critical systems for large, multinational organizations."

### Primary CTA
"Our Services"

### Color palette
Not captured — needs browser visit.

### Font stack
Not captured — needs browser visit.

### What to steal
**"Boutique, owner-operated" + "mission-critical."** This is the most powerful positioning language in their entire site and it's buried in the subheadline. They discovered this territory and then underused it. TMT SA LLC should inherit and amplify it — "owner-operated" signals executive accountability that no $50M-funded platform can replicate. Every decision Marco makes is his name on the line. That is a genuine differentiation and it should be in the hero, not the footer.

**30 years of credibility anchor.** They lead with tenure. We can't copy the number but we can copy the format — lead with a proof anchor in the hero. Placement count, retention rate, or delivery timeline. A concrete number that says "we have a track record."

### What to exploit
**"Our Services" is arguably the worst primary CTA in enterprise B2B.** It points inward, not at the buyer's outcome. It says "look at what we do" not "here's what you get." A CTO who lands on this page and clicks "Our Services" is already doing work. Every CTA on TMT SA LLC's site points at a buyer outcome or a booking — never inward.

**The headline has zero specificity.** "Quality, security, and long-term reliability" describes every professional services firm on earth. A law firm, an accountant, a plumber — all claim these three things. There is no differentiation in the hero. Their actual differentiator (boutique, owner-operated, mission-critical, 30 years) is in the subheadline where most buyers won't reach. TMT SA LLC leads with the sharp claim, not the generic one.

**Visually outdated.** Their aesthetic reads 2015 corporate. For a firm selling to modern SaaS CTOs who operate in dark-mode IDEs, the visual language is a mismatch. The Surgical Elite aesthetic immediately signals "these people live in the same technical environment I do."

## 04 — INCLUSION CLOUD
**URL:** inclusioncloud.com
**Model:** Enterprise AI factory

### Hero headline
> "Engineering the AI-Driven Enterprise"

### Subheadline
"We help enterprises rethink, accelerate, and build the next generation of AI-native enterprise solutions."

### Primary CTA
"Get AI Talent Fast"

### Color palette
Not captured — needs browser visit.

### Font stack
Not captured — needs browser visit.

### What to steal
**"Get AI Talent Fast" is the strongest CTA in the set.** It's direct, benefit-led, and specific. The word "Fast" does real work — it's what the buyer wants. Our equivalent needs to be equally direct but pivot the value axis from speed to precision: "Book a Discovery Call" is fine but "Find Your Top 3% Engineer" or "Start the Vetting Process" are stronger because they describe the outcome, not the meeting.

**The "AI-Driven" positioning is smart timing.** They recognized the buyer anxiety shift — CTOs aren't just buying engineers, they're buying AI capability. Note this and consider whether TMT SA LLC should acknowledge AI/ML expertise as a specialty in the salary guide and compliance page. It's where enterprise budget is flowing.

### What to exploit
**"Engineering the AI-Driven Enterprise" is pure buzzword.** Remove "AI-Driven" and replace with any tech trend of the past 10 years and the sentence still works — "Engineering the Cloud-Native Enterprise," "Engineering the Digital Enterprise." It's trend-chasing, not positioning. It tells you nothing about what they actually do, where their talent comes from, or why you should trust them. By Day 25, this headline will feel generic to the same buyers they're targeting.

**The subheadline is even worse.** "Rethink, accelerate, and build" — three verbs that mean nothing without a specific object. Enterprise buyers who have been burned by vendor promises read this and feel nothing. TMT SA LLC's copy says exactly what we do, to whom, and why it protects you. Specificity over aspiration, every time.

**They've abandoned their nearshore positioning for AI positioning.** Six months ago their differentiator was LatAm talent at enterprise quality. Now they're an "AI solutions" firm. That repositioning creates a vacuum in the precision nearshore market that TMT SA LLC can fill with a clear, unwavering message.
